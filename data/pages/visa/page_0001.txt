Visa Card Usage and Features

Identity Theft:

If your identity has been stolen:
- Contact your card issuer immediately to freeze your account
- File a police report
- Place a fraud alert with credit bureaus
- Monitor your credit report for unauthorized activity
- Consider identity theft protection services
- Visa's Zero Liability policy protects you from unauthorized charges

Cash Advances:

Getting cash with your Visa card:
- Use ATMs displaying the Visa logo
- Cash advances typically have fees (3-5% of amount)
- Interest starts accruing immediately (no grace period)
- Check with your card issuer for cash advance limits
- Alternative: Use Visa Direct for instant transfers (if supported)

Minimum Purchase Requirements:

Regarding minimum purchase amounts:
- In the US and territories, merchants cannot set minimums for credit cards
- For debit cards, merchants can set minimums up to $10
- This applies to US Virgin Islands
- If a merchant violates this rule, report to your card issuer
- Provide merchant name, location, and date

Card Blocked While Traveling:

If your card is blocked during travel:
- Contact your card issuer's international helpline
- Number is on the back of your card or issuer's website
- Fraud prevention systems may flag unusual location activity
- Always notify your bank before international travel
- Keep issuer's contact number saved separately from your card
